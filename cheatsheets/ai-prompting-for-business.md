# AI Prompting for Business — Quick Reference

How to get useful output from Claude, ChatGPT, or any AI tool for actual business tasks.

## The Formula

```
ROLE + CONTEXT + TASK + CONSTRAINTS + FORMAT
```

**Bad:** "Write me an email."
**Good:** "Act as my operations manager. I need to follow up with a hotel contact about missing AV documentation for our April conference. Write a direct, 3-sentence email asking for the AV quote by Friday."

## 5 Prompts Every Business Owner Needs

### 1. The Email Drafter
```
Write a [tone] email to [recipient] about [topic].
Context: [relevant background].
Keep it under [X] words. End with [specific ask].
```

### 2. The Meeting Prep
```
I have a meeting with [person/org] about [topic] in [time].
Their priorities are probably: [what they care about].
Give me: 3 talking points, 2 questions to ask, and 1 thing to avoid saying.
```

### 3. The Decision Maker
```
I need to decide between [option A] and [option B].
What matters to me: [criteria].
Build a comparison table scoring each option 1-10 on each criterion.
Be honest — don't make them look equal if they aren't.
```

### 4. The Content Batcher
```
Create [number] [platform] posts for [brand/account].
Rotate through these themes: [theme 1, theme 2, theme 3].
Tone: [how it should sound].
Avoid: [what not to do].
Include hashtags and image suggestions.
```

### 5. The Process Documenter
```
I just did [process] and I need to write it up so someone else can do it next time.
Here's roughly what I did: [brain dump].
Turn this into a numbered step-by-step SOP.
Flag anywhere I should add a screenshot or example.
```

## Power Moves

### Make It Shorter
> "Cut this in half. Every word must earn its place."

### Make It Honest
> "Be a blunt advisor who cares more about saving me time than being polite."

### Make It Specific
> "Don't give me generic advice. Use the specific details I gave you."

### Make It Actionable
> "End every recommendation with the exact next step I should take."

### Make It Better
> "Review what you just wrote. Score it 1-10 on [criteria]. List 3 improvements. Then rewrite it."

## What AI Is Good At (Use It For)

- Drafting emails and communications
- Summarizing long documents
- Brainstorming and generating options
- Reformatting data
- Creating templates and checklists
- Research and comparison
- Cleaning messy data

## What AI Is Bad At (Don't Trust It For)

- Current facts without web search (it guesses)
- Legal or financial advice (always verify)
- Anything involving your private data (be careful what you paste)
- Replacing your judgment on important decisions
- Creative work that needs YOUR voice (it gives you a starting point, not the final product)
